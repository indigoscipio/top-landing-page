SPQR, an abbreviation for Senātus Populusque Rōmānus (Classical Latin: [s̠ɛnät̪ʊs̠ pɔpʊɫ̪ʊs̠kʷɛ romänʊs̠]; English: "The Roman Senate and People"; or more freely The Senate and People of Rome"), is an emblematic abbreviated phrase referring to the government of the ancient Roman Republic. It appears on Roman currency, at the end of documents made public by an inscription in stone or metal, and in dedications of monuments and public and civil works.
